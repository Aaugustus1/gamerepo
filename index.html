Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about streaming from The Game Repository.

No downloads required. All games stream directly to your browser via our cloud infrastructure. As long as you have a modern browser (Chrome, Safari, Firefox, Edge) and a stable internet connection of at least 10 Mbps, you can play instantly.

Access to the standard streaming queue is free. A short sponsor verification step is required to skip the queue and gain immediate access to a server node — this helps us cover server costs and keep the platform free for everyone.

TGR works on any device with a modern browser — including Windows PCs, Macs, Android phones, and iPhones. For the best experience on mobile, tap Install App in the navigation bar to add TGR to your home screen and play in full-screen mode.

Yes. TGR supports both physical gamepads (Xbox, PlayStation, and generic USB/Bluetooth controllers) via the browser Gamepad API, as well as virtual on-screen touch controls for mobile users. Simply plug in your controller before launching a session.

Our GPU-powered server nodes have a finite capacity. During peak hours, demand can exceed available slots. The queue ensures fair access — and you can always skip it instantly by completing the short sponsor step, which funds additional server capacity.

We recommend a minimum of 10 Mbps for 720p streaming and 25 Mbps for 1080p at 60 FPS. For 4K / 120 FPS sessions, 50 Mbps or above delivers the best experience. You can test your speed at Fast.com before launching.

The Game Repository operates as a digital preservation archive under educational fair-use provisions. Legacy and discontinued titles are archived for research and historical access. Modern titles in the Premium Cloud Library are licensed through their respective publishers.

Save states are stored per-session on the server node. For Premium Cloud Library titles, cloud saves persist across sessions when you are signed in to your TGR account. Legacy and archive titles support in-session save states only.
